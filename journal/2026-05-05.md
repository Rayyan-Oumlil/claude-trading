## Backtest — Phase C: VIX regime gate

**Hypothesis:** Restricting MA-crossover entries to bars where `VIX < 25` should reduce false breakouts during high-vol chop, improving risk-adjusted return and lowering drawdown.

**Pre-committed decision rule (written before running the backtest):**
> Keep the gate if **Sharpe improves AND drawdown drops AND trades/year stays ≥ 3**. Otherwise discard. No re-tuning of the threshold.

### Verdict: **DISCARD the VIX25 gate.**

The pre-committed rule fails on every dimension:
- Sharpe did **not** improve (IS regressed, OOS unchanged at 3 decimals).
- Max drawdown barely changed (a 25 bps tightening IS, none OOS).
- Trades/year dropped further below 3 — the original soft-fail got worse.

The thesis ("VIX > 25 means signal noise") did not survive contact with SPY 10/50 daily over 2015–2024. The signals that fire when VIX > 25 are apparently still useful — likely because a fast-MA cross-up *during* high-vol chop is a re-entry after a flush, and those have positive expectancy in this dataset.

### What I am NOT doing

- Re-tuning to VIX 20 or 30. Per CLAUDE.md §7 and PRINCIPLES.md corollary "Don't tweak in a loop." Two parameter passes max; we have one (the literature default) and it failed.
- Inventing a new filter to rescue the gate. That is the sunk-cost trap.

The gate is dead. Moving on.

## VIX gate sanity check (follow-up, same session)

Diffed the trade lists between baseline OOS (9 trades) and VIX25 OOS (8 trades). **Exactly one trade** was filtered:

| Entry | Exit | PnL | Return | Reason |
|---|---|---:|---:|---|
| 2022-11-04 | 2022-12-27 | **+$1,763.36** | **+2.00%** | signal exit |

The gate dropped a **winner**, not a loser. That fully explains the empirical result: OOS Sharpe and max DD unchanged, returns lower, win rate lower (66.7% → 62.5%) because removing a winner shrinks the numerator of the win-rate ratio.

**Why this trade got filtered:** SPY put in its bear-market low on 2022-10-13 (~$348). The MA(10/50) cross-up triggered on 2022-11-04 at $359.94. VIX in early November 2022 was running elevated (>25) on CPI/FOMC headline risk — so the gate said "noisy regime, skip." But the late-2022 rally was a real reversal, not chop, and the MA crossover correctly caught it.

**Lesson worth keeping:** High VIX + a fresh fast-MA cross-up is often a **reversal entry**, not a false breakout. The thesis "high VIX = bad signal" is the wrong frame for trend-following entries on broad-index ETFs. This is structural, not parameter-tuning. Threshold 30 wouldn't fix it; the wrong-frame-ness is independent of the number.

This is a good outcome: the discipline (pre-committed rule, no re-tuning) prevented us from shipping a filter that would have cost us money.
