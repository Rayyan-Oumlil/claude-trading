## Surprises
1. **Strategy underperforms buy-and-hold in raw return**, both IS (72% vs 164%) and OOS (21% vs 29%). This is characteristic of trend-following on equity indices — you pay for smaller drawdown with missed returns during choppy uptrends.
2. **Drawdown is much lower than B&H would suggest** — the stop and signal exit do their job. This is the actual value: not higher returns, but lower pain.
3. **Trade count is low** (~3 per year). SPY doesn't flip 10/50 very often. Makes statistical significance weaker than ideal.
4. **First backtest had a serious accounting bug** — I didn't deduct position cost from cash on entry, which made the equity curve include phantom capital. Caught it in review when max DD looked suspiciously high (-53%). Lesson for lessons.md: always mark-to-market properly, cash + position_value, never just equity + position_value.
5. **IS and OOS Sharpe diverged by 0.18** (0.82 → 0.65). Some decay but nothing to flag as overfit — the strategy has 2 parameters, both from literature, so there's not much to overfit on.

## Decision
Proceed to Phase 3 (Alpaca paper wiring) and Phase 4 (routines) with the MA crossover. Not because it beats SPY — it doesn't — but because:
- It passes the hard gates (Sharpe, drawdown).
- The purpose of Stage 2 paper trading is to validate the **plumbing**, not to maximize return. Paper is about: does the signal produce the right orders? Does the stop trigger correctly? Does the journal write back? Does the kill switch work?
- Once plumbing is proven, swap in a better strategy without rebuilding infrastructure.

The trade count soft-fail is noted. If paper trading shows <3 trades in 2 weeks, I'll re-evaluate before live.
